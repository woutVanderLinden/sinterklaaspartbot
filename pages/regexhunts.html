RegEx Hunts


Welcome to RegEx hunts! These are a fairly new type of hunt, and once you get what they mean, they're rather fun. The premise is simple - answer with the shortest RegEx that would match all of the given Pokemon (or moves) - and only the given Pokemon (or moves), no others.

First up: what is RegEx? RegEx, in the simplest terms, is a way to find or filter pieces of text. Stuff like 'all things that include an E followed by a C' or 'a vowel followed by two consonants, then a number'. It's an elegant system for searching - even if certain RegExes look scary to the eye.

Answering


Sadly, we can't let people answer directly with the RegEx. Why? The reason is PS's validator - it removes all punctuation while comparing answers. Which means that ^ha is an acceptable answer for ha.?[^\.!]$. Since that would make it gameable, there's a slight extra rule - replace all punctuation with the corresponding numbers, given below.
^ 33
. 0
+ 39
$ 30
( or )603
[ or ]600
{ or }69
? 609
\ 66
| 36
: 606
- 63
* 9

For example,
^(k|r).*\1$
would be answered as
33 603 k 36 r 603 0 9 66 1 30
.
Additionally, whenever there's a list (whether it's of numbers or letters), arrange the terms alphabetically. So a RegEx like
(d|ef|bca)
would be answered with the code for
(bca|d|ef)
, which is
603 bca 36 d 36 ef 603
. Similarly,
[ahgcv]
would be answered with the code for
[acghv]
.
